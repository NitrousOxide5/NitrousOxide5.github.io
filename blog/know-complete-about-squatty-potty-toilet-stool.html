How Squatty Potty Actually Works-

To understand how the Squatty Potty works, it is important to understand the human digestive system first. As food reaches the stomach it is moved to the small intestine for nutrient absorption and excretion of the waste. The excreta stays in a large pouch at the end of the large intestine known as the rectum. When it’s time to poop, the rectum contracts and the puborectalis muscle relaxes helping in excretion.

When a person defecates sitting normally the rectum isn’t fully straight which can cause problems especially for those with constipation. This is one of the major causes of colon blockage leading to serious problems like hemorrhoids.

The stool aims at removing the kink created in the rectum area with a straight posture. The stool lets the users put their legs on the stool which makes the knees stay above the stomach area resembling a squatting position.

According to a study in Japan, researchers found that while squatting the anorectal angle increases significantly from 100 degrees to 126 degrees which makes the elimination process easier. The creators of the toilet stools used this insight to make a product that helped people who are not habitual to squatting. Here are some factors that contribute to making the toilet stools effective for excretion assistance.

  • When a person uses a squatting position, the weight of the torso combines with gravity and forms a natural pressure on the colon. This helps in the completion of the excretion process without minimum straining on both the gut and rectum.
  • The puborectalis muscles which play a vital role in the elimination process, get further relaxed with the squatting position with the betterment of the anorectal angle.
  • The sigmoid colon rests in the normal pooping position creating pressure on the puborectalis muscles. While squatting the sigmoid colon gets lifted, opening the kink at the end of the rectum.
  • A straight posture does not help with the closing and opening of the ileocecal valve and the puborectalis muscle which is necessary for proper bowel movement. Whereas the squatting position mainly focuses on this making it easier with the help of the poop stool.
